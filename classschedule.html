M/W/F SCHEDULE

Instructor: Professor Terry Hill

Time: 10 AM - 10:50 AM

This course examines fundamental aspects of life at the level of cells and molecules. Major themes addressed in the course include the nature of biologically important molecules and how that relates to the structural organization of cells, the cellular mechanisms of heredity and reproduction; 3) mechanisms by which cell functions are regulated, and the central pathways of metabolic energy conversions. Throughout the course, strong emphasis will be placed upon the relationship between complexity of function and complexity of structure, as well as upon the importance of energy in any process having complex outcomes.

Instructor: Brian Larkins

Time: 11 AM - 11:50 AM

A bottom-up exploration of the interaction between computer hardware and software. Topics begin with an introduction to digital logic, and continue through elementary processor design, to assembly language, machine data representation, and computer arithmetic. Other topics in contemporary computer architecture such as multicore processors and GPGPU’s may be discussed as time allows. Projects include hardware and simulated digital circuits as well as programs in assembly language.

Instructor: Professor Christopher Mouron

Time: 12 PM - 12:50 PM

An introduction to and survey of the mathematics used in computer science including functions, relations, sets, counting, logic, Boolean algebra, proof techniques, induction, recursion, computational complexity, and computability. Other topics may be included as time permits.
